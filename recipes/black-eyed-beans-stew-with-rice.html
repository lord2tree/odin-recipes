Ingredients

  • 2 cups of black-eyed beans
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp ginger, grated
  • 1-2 chili peppers, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon of paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 can (400 ml) coconut milk
  • 3 cups of water or vegetable stock
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups of rice
  • 4 cups of water
  • For serving: Spinach or collard greens, washed and chopped

Steps

  1. Rinse the black-eyed beans and soak them overnight in cold water. Drain and rinse before cooking.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and finely chopped chili pepper to the pot and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add the smoked paprika, cumin, and coriander to the pot and stir well.
  5. Add the tomato paste, coconut milk, water or vegetable stock, and the soaked black-eyed beans to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cover and let simmer for 1-1.5 hours until the beans are tender.
  6. While the beans are cooking, rinse the rice thoroughly in cold water. Add the rice and 4 cups of water to a pot and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat, cover, and let simmer for 18-20 minutes until the rice is tender and the water has been absorbed.
  7. Once the black-eyed beans are tender, season with salt and pepper to taste. Add the chopped spinach or collard greens to the pot and let cook for another 2-3 minutes until wilted.
  8. Serve the black-eyed beans stew with the rice, garnished with additional chopped greens, if desired.
