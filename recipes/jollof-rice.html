Ingredients

  • 2 cups of long-grain white rice
  • 4 cups of water or chicken stock
  • 1/2 cup of tomato paste
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of dried rosemary
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable oil or any other oil of your choice
  • 2 pounds of bone-in chicken or beef (optional)
  • 2-3 cups of mixed vegetables (such as bell peppers, carrots, and peas)

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice thoroughly with cold water and drain.
  2. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger and continue to sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the tomato paste, paprika, cayenne pepper, thyme, rosemary, bay leaves, salt, and black pepper. Cook the spices and tomato paste for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. If using chicken or beef, add it to the pot and cook until it is browned on all sides.
  6. Pour in the water or chicken stock, add the mixed vegetables, and bring the mixture to a boil.
  7. Add the rinsed rice to the pot and stir everything together.
  8. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and reduce the heat to low.
  9. Cook the Jollof rice for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid has been absorbed.
  10. Once the rice is cooked, remove the pot from the heat and let it sit covered for another 5 minutes to allow the rice to steam.
  11. Remove the bay leaves and fluff the rice with a fork.
  12. Serve the Jollof rice hot and garnish with fresh herbs or additional vegetables if desired.
